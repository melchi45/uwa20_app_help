Image Alignment

You can set the field of view and detection distance for your mounted camera.

Note

  • Field of view and detection distance cannot be set simultaneously.

Angular field of view

When you arrange the videos of mounted cameras to create a panoramic view of the videos, you can adjust the field of view by choosing the way of arranging each individual camera mounted.

  • 180 degree mode: Arrange camera screens vertically. Since this mode arranges the screens according to the length of each screen, you will see a video whose length is longer than that in the 220 degree mode.  
  • 220 degree mode: Arrange camera screens horizontally. Since this mode arranges the screens according to the width of each screen, you will see a video whose width is longer than that in the 180 degree mode.

Note

  • If you change the field of view and click the [OK] button at the bottom of the page, your cameras will reboot and the video setup will be reset to the selected field of view. If you have set [Analytics] and [Statistics], change the settings in [Analytics] and [Statistics] menus so that you can analyze videos or accumulate statistical data in accordance with the changed video settings.

Target distance

After selecting the field of view of your camera, you can enter a distance value for the target object that is being monitored. You can set the value by adjusting the slide bar or enter a value in meters.

An LUT (Look Up Table) will be generated according to the entered distance value and applied to the panoramic view.
